How To Play

Tic-tac-toe (American English), noughts and crosses (Commonwealth English), or Xs and Os (Irish English) is a paper-and-pencil
game for two players who take turns marking the spaces in a three-by-three grid with X or O. The player who succeeds in placing
three of their marks in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row is the winner. It is a solved game, with a forced draw assuming
best play from both players.

Player 1 and player 2 take turns clicking on the 9 boxes of tic tac toe. Note that right after the button has been clicked by a
player, it will be disabled which means that it cannot be clicked again by either of the players. The symbol will automatically
switch between O and X after every click.

When the same symbol (either O or X) connects in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal line, that user wins. The game is over and
the results are displayed.
